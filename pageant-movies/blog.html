Some records are made in spite of their surroundings. The best rooms are often the accidents — the friend's basement that turned out to have a peculiar reverb, the closet that became an isolation booth out of necessity. A meditation on acoustics, accident, and the sounds that end up staying.

A logbook of sorts, kept across fourteen cities and eleven weeks. The diner at 11am. The wrong exit. The twenty minutes before soundcheck where everything feels possible before the usual obstacles reassert themselves. What touring teaches you about patience, about talking to strangers, about the same song sung in sixty different rooms.
